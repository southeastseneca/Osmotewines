      <section id="about">
        <p class="section-label">About</p>
        <h2 class="section-title">Osmote Wine</h2>
        <p>Osmote is one of the most distinctive small wineries in the Finger Lakes — family-run, lake-shaped, and committed to producing balanced, low-impact wines that express the unique character of Seneca Lake without being forced into it. Winemaker Ben Riccardi holds a degree in oenology from Cornell University and spent multiple vintages in New Zealand and the south of France before bringing that experience back to Burdett.</p>
        <p>The name Osmote is an homage to the moderating influence the lake exerts on the surrounding vineyards — the same force that keeps vines alive through brutal upstate winters and extends the growing season into autumn. Under 24,000 bottles are produced each year, sourced from carefully selected Seneca Lake vineyards alongside Osmote's own estate blocks planted in 2024 at 950 feet above sea level on the eastern shore.</p>
        <p>The tasting experience is centered around a stunning timber-framed pavilion and slate stone patio overlooking panoramic Seneca Lake views and the farm below. Tastings begin on the hour, with guest numbers capped each session to ensure a genuine, intimate experience. Appointments are preferred. The wines — ranging from their beloved Pet Nat to Chardonnay, Cabernet Franc, Riesling, and hybrid varieties — have earned spots on Michelin-starred restaurant lists worldwide.</p>
      </section>

        <p class="story-label"><span class="ses-icon" style="width:24px;height:24px;background:rgba(238,175,112,0.2);"><svg style="width:13px;height:13px;"><use href="#icon-pen" style="stroke:var(--amber)"/></svg></span>In Our Words</p>
        <h3 class="story-title">Our Story</h3>
        <div class="story-body">
          <p>Osmote earns its reputation quietly — no tasting bar theatrics, no slick branding, just wines that are genuinely alive and a setting that rewards the trip. The pavilion is one of the most beautiful tasting spots on the east shore: timber-framed, open to the lake, and surrounded by the farm that makes it all possible.</p>
          <p>The Pet Nat alone is worth the drive. It's become a Finger Lakes icon — pure orchard fruit, natural carbonation, that signature salty yellow plum finish. But the Chardonnay, the Cab Franc, and the hybrid blends show just how broadly talented Ben Riccardi is. This is small-production, serious winemaking that doesn't take itself too seriously. Exactly the kind of place that makes the east shore special.</p>

      <section id="wines">
        <p class="section-label">The Wines</p>
        <h2 class="section-title">What's Pouring</h2>
        <p>Under 24,000 bottles produced annually — each wine a small-batch expression of Seneca Lake terroir, naturally fermented and built around balance you can feel.</p>
        <ul class="wine-list">
          <li><strong>This Is Pet Nat ★</strong><span>The Osmote signature — crisp, natural sparkling with orchard fruit and that salty yellow plum finish. A Finger Lakes icon.</span></li>
          <li><strong>This Is Red Pet Nat</strong><span>Carbonic maceration Pet Nat — pomegranate, strawberry, violet, and earthy warmth. Disgorged under crown cap.</span></li>
          <li><strong>Chardonnay</strong><span>Cool-climate Chardonnay with orchard fruit, crème brûlée, and a clean, bright finish — elegant and food-friendly.</span></li>
          <li><strong>Riesling</strong><span>Balanced dry and semi-dry Riesling with a vein of minerality, lime zest, and taut acidity that defines the lake.</span></li>
          <li><strong>Cabernet Franc</strong><span>Light-bodied with cranberry, raspberry, black pepper, and florals — whole cluster fermented, built for versatility.</span></li>
          <li><strong>TTYL White Blend</strong><span>A wildly aromatic white blend — Tocai Friuliano and Traminette with skin contact, tropical fruit, and a dry rounded body.</span></li>
          <li><strong>DeChaunac</strong><span>A precious heirloom hybrid — bright red berry, black currant leaf, and a unique oak-fermented expression exclusive to Osmote.</span></li>
          <li><strong>Pocket Wine</strong><span>Osmote's approachable everyday pour — ask at the tasting for what's current in the lineup.</span></li>
        </ul>
